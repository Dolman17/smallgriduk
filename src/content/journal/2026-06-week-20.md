## This week’s goal

Write down how the setup actually works.

Not polished documentation. Not a manual. Just enough notes that future me does not have to reverse-engineer my own decisions like a confused detective.

## What I actually did

I documented:

- server IP address
- service URLs
- important folders
- backup locations
- restore steps
- update routine
- storage mount points
- what not to touch casually

The last category may be the most important.

The notes are plain, boring, and useful. That is the correct order.

## What broke, or nearly did

I found several things I had definitely planned to remember.

I had not remembered them.

This was both predictable and, somehow, still disappointing.

## What I’m keeping

- One simple notes file
- Commands copied exactly
- Service list with ports
- Recovery steps written in human language

## SmallGrid takeaway

Documentation does not need to be beautiful. It needs to exist before something breaks. Future you is not a reliable storage system.
